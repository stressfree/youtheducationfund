The J R McKenzie Youth Education Fund

The late Sir John McKenzie so appreciated the success of McKenzies stores in this country and the support given him by the people of New Zealand, that he founded the Youth Education Trust Fund to help deserving young people to receive education.

Objective

The J R McKenzie Youth Education Fund was set up in 1938, its main purpose being:

"to assist in the betterment, education, advancement and physical welfare of the youth in the community, particularly in cases where bereavement, sickness or family disturbances leave the children in need of assistance if they are to continue their studies".

Since its inception, thousands of young New Zealanders have received assistance.

Criteria

The Fund wishes to help where death, sickness, family break up or economic hardship cause difficulty for young people and their caregivers to meet the costs of their education. Applications must be related to a young person's education, although this term can be broadly interpreted. The Fund will not cover general tuition fees, unless there are unusual circumstances.

Types of Grants Made

Depending upon local criteria, grants may cover:

  1. School uniforms and clothing and footwear.
  2. Special tuition or course fees.
  3. Text books and stationery.
  4. Special grants especially for glasses or to help students with special needs.

Income for Distribution

The income of the fund comes from dividends and interest, and an annual grant from the J R McKenzie Trust. This income tends to vary, dependent upon the success of Rangitira Pty Ltd, as the Fund and the Trust have substantial shareholdings in this Company

Administration

The Fund is administered entirely by the Rotary clubs of North Harbour, Auckland, Hamilton, Wellington, Christchurch and Dunedin. These clubs have a separate committee who receive and consider all applications from the individuals (through Rotarians) other Rotary clubs, community organizations, school principals, ministers and social workers. After considering the degree of hardship and seeing evidence of a degree of self-help, they distribute grants to the most deserving cases through the nearest Rotary Club, who can discuss problems and offer advice, if requested. Applications are invited around September of each year, but can be made at any time.

Other McKenzie Trusts

The J R McKenzie Youth Education Fund is one of the trusts established by the McKenzie Family.
The J R McKenzie Trust is one of New Zealand's largest trusts and provides grants totalling approximately $3 million annually to nearly 1,000 community groups and national organisations.

Contact details and further information about the Trust are available on its website.
